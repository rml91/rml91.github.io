Research

Conformal Gravity & Cosmological implications

We investigate the cosmological predictions of the Conformal Higgs Model (CHM) proposed by Nesbet (2023), with particular focus on relaxing the static Higgs field assumption $\phi(t) = \phi_0$ that underlies the original analysis. We first reproduce Nesbet's Table I \cite{nesbet2023} luminosity distance results and verify the conformal sum rule $\Omega_\Lambda + \Omega_q = 1$ for redshifts $z \leq 1$, confirming agreement with the standard $\Lambda$CDM fit using a single free parameter $\alpha = \Omega_\Lambda(t_0) = 0.732$. We then evaluate the Ricci scalar $R(z)$ and introduce a diagnostic $D(z)$ to quantify the relative importance of time-derivative terms in the scalar field equation, establishing the redshift at which Nesbet's static approximation breaks down. Finally, we solve the fully coupled dynamical system for the scale factor $a(t)$ and Higgs field $\phi(t)$ simultaneously, without assuming $\phi(t) = \phi_0$, by implementing the gauge-field dressing condition $w^2(z) = (1+z)^2 H^2 (\phi'/\phi)^2$ self-consistently. The resulting dynamical CHM predictions for $H(z)$, the density parameters $\Omega_\Lambda(z)$, $\Omega_q(z)$, and the luminosity distance $H_0 d_L/c$ are compared against both the Nesbet static approximation and the standard $\Lambda$CDM model. Our results quantify the corrections to conformal gravity cosmology arising from Higgs field dynamics and extend the validity of CHM predictions to higher redshifts beyond $z \leq 1$ (hopefully).

Formalisation of electrical networks for the dounling map

Classical results show that for finite, reversible Markov chains, probabilistic quantities expressed via electrical analogies: voltages, currents, and effective resistances. We extend these methods to Markov partitions of dynamical systems encoded as topological Markov chains. De Bruijn graphs can model these sequences equipped with non-uniform Bernoulli measures. We transform these irreversible processes into electrical networks, building on the work of Doyle-Snell and Chandra for probabilistic interpretations, Gaudillière-Landim-Slowik for the ex- tension of the Dirichlet principle, and finally Balasz-Folly for their amplifiers. This is done by perturbing (and renormalising) the transi- tion probabilities and computing the commute times before taking the limit of the perturbation to zero. The effective resistance will be used to define a metric corresponding to a dynamical distance on the dis- crete space state of linear expanding maps of the circle, in particular, the doubling map.
