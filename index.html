Research

Conformal Gravity & Cosmological implications

Study of conformal gravity models and their relation to holographic dualities. We investigate the cosmological predictions of the Conformal Higgs Model (CHM) proposed by Nesbet (2023), with particular focus on relaxing the static Higgs field assumption $\phi(t) = \phi_0$ that underlies the original analysis. We first reproduce Nesbet's Table I \cite{nesbet2023} luminosity distance results and verify the conformal sum rule $\Omega_\Lambda + \Omega_q = 1$ for redshifts $z \leq 1$, confirming agreement with the standard $\Lambda$CDM fit using a single free parameter $\alpha = \Omega_\Lambda(t_0) = 0.732$. We then evaluate the Ricci scalar $R(z)$ and introduce a diagnostic $D(z)$ to quantify the relative importance of time-derivative terms in the scalar field equation, establishing the redshift at which Nesbet's static approximation breaks down. Finally, we solve the fully coupled dynamical system for the scale factor $a(t)$ and Higgs field $\phi(t)$ simultaneously, without assuming $\phi(t) = \phi_0$, by implementing the gauge-field dressing condition $w^2(z) = (1+z)^2 H^2 (\phi'/\phi)^2$ self-consistently. The resulting dynamical CHM predictions for $H(z)$, the density parameters $\Omega_\Lambda(z)$, $\Omega_q(z)$, and the luminosity distance $H_0 d_L/c$ are compared against both the Nesbet static approximation and the standard $\Lambda$CDM model. Our results quantify the corrections to conformal gravity cosmology arising from Higgs field dynamics and extend the validity of CHM predictions to higher redshifts beyond $z \leq 1$ (hopefully).
Supervision: Prof. Keith Horne
